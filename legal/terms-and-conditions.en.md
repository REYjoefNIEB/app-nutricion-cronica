## 3. GENETIC ANALYSIS

### 3.1 Purpose

Nura's genetic analysis has an **informational and preventive** purpose. It aims to help you make better lifestyle decisions in consultation with healthcare professionals.

### 3.2 What it is NOT

- **NOT a clinical test.** Only CAP/CLIA-certified clinical laboratories can issue diagnoses.
- **NOT a cancer diagnosis.** Any oncogenetic finding requires confirmation in a clinical laboratory and evaluation by an oncogeneticist.
- **NOT a basis for medical decisions.** Do not start, change, or stop treatments based solely on Nura results.
- **NOT a replacement for family history.** Complete risk assessment requires medical genealogy.

### 3.3 Known technical limitations

- We use SNP chip files (23andMe, AncestryDNA, MyHeritage)
- These chips analyze ~600,000–1,000,000 variants (out of ~3 billion total)
- We do not detect rare variants not included in the chip
- We do not detect structural variants (CNVs, inversions, large deletions)
